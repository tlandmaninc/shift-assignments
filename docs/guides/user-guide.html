App Overview

How the app is organized and who can access what

What This App Does

The Shift Assignment platform helps the Psychiatry Department manage shift scheduling fairly and efficiently. It covers the entire workflow:

Collect Availability

Create Google Forms to gather employee availability for each month.

Generate Assignments

AI-powered algorithm assigns shifts fairly based on constraints and history.

Track Fairness

Analytics dashboard shows shift distribution, trends, and fairness scores.

Exchange Shifts

Employees can request shift swaps with real-time notifications.

Ask the AI

Chat assistant answers questions about schedules, assignments, and fairness.

Calendar Export

Add shifts to Google Calendar with a single click from your profile.

User Roles

There are two roles in the app. Your role is determined by your email address:

Admin

Full access to all pages. Can create forms, generate assignments, manage employees, view analytics, and control page visibility for other users.

Employee

Access to Dashboard, Profile, Shift Exchange, Chat, and History (if enabled by admin). Can view their own shifts and request exchanges.

Navigation

The app has a collapsible sidebar on the left and a top header bar:

  • Sidebar — Click the arrow at the bottom to collapse/expand. Shows all pages you have access to.
  • Header — Shows the banner, your avatar, dark mode toggle, and notification bell.
  • Notifications — Click the bell icon to see recent updates like published shifts or exchange requests.
  • Dark Mode — Click the sun/moon icon in the header to toggle between light and dark themes.

Two Ways to Sign In

Google Sign-In

Click "Sign in with Google" and choose your Google account. This is the fastest method — one click and you're in.

Phone Number

Click "Sign in with Phone Number", enter your Clalit email (@clalit.org.il) and Israeli phone number, then verify the SMS code.

Phone Login Steps

  1. Enter Your Details

    Type your Clalit email (must end in @clalit.org.il) and your phone number in the format 05X-XXX-XXXX.

  2. Receive SMS Code

    Click "Send Code". You'll receive a 6-digit verification code via SMS within a few seconds.

  3. Enter the Code

    Type the 6-digit code and click "Verify Code". If the code expired, click "Resend Code" (available after 60 seconds).

If you see "rate limited", wait a minute and try again. The system allows a limited number of SMS codes per minute.

The Assignment Wizard (4 Steps)

A guided process to go from employee responses to published shifts. A progress bar at the top tracks your current step with numbered circles and checkmarks.

Step 1: Select a Form

Choose the month to assign shifts for. Each form card shows the title, shift type badge (e.g., ECT), date count, and status. Click a card to proceed.

Step 1 — Select a form. The March 2026 ECT form shows as "active" with 19 scheduled dates.
Only forms with status "Active" can be used for new assignments. Already-processed forms show as "Processed" and are greyed out.

Step 2: Import Employee Availability

Choose how to get the employee data:

Step 2 — Two import methods: paste CSV from Google Sheets, or fetch directly from a linked Google Form.

Fetch from Google Forms

If a Google Form is linked, click "Fetch from Google Forms". The app reads all submissions automatically via the Google API.

Paste CSV Data

Open your Google Form responses in Google Sheets, select all data (Ctrl+A), copy (Ctrl+C), and paste into the text area. The parser auto-detects employee names, dates, and availability.

CSV data pasted — 10 employee responses with availability for 19 dates. Click "Parse CSV" to continue.

Step 3: Review & Validate

The app validates the imported data and displays a summary:

Step 3 — All 10 employees parsed and validated. Green banner confirms data is ready for assignment.
  • Validation status — green check ("Data is valid and ready for assignment") or red errors to fix
  • Employee cards — each card shows the employee name, New/Experienced badge, and count of available dates
  • Warnings — non-blocking issues like low availability counts

Click "Generate Assignments" to run the scheduling algorithm.

How the Algorithm Works
The scheduler uses a fairness-first approach with backtracking constraint satisfaction. It considers each employee's total historical shifts, their availability, and hard constraints (max 2 shifts/month, no consecutive days, no same weekday twice, new employees limited to last 2 weeks) to find the most balanced assignment.

Step 4: Results & Publish

After the algorithm runs, you'll see a success banner with the shift type and count, a full calendar preview, and a shift distribution table.

Step 4 — "Assignments Generated Successfully!" with a color-coded calendar showing all 19 ECT shifts assigned to 10 employees.

The Shift Distribution table below the calendar shows how shifts are balanced across employees:

Shift Distribution — Each employee's shift count and experience level. Fair distribution: most employees receive 2 shifts.

Charts & Visualizations

The Analytics Dashboard includes 6 interactive chart tabs. Click any tab to switch views. Hover any data point for details.

Use the sort dropdown (above charts) to reorder employees by shift count (ascending/descending) or alphabetically.

1. Employee Trends

A line chart plotting each employee's cumulative shift count over time. Each employee is a separate colored line.

Employee Trends — Track how each employee's shift load evolves month by month
  • X-axis — Months (December 2025, January 2026, etc.)
  • Y-axis — Number of shifts assigned
  • Lines — One line per employee, color-coded. Hover a line to see the exact count.
  • What to look for — Lines should rise at similar rates. If one line climbs much faster, that employee is getting more than their fair share.

2. Monthly Overview

A dual-pane synchronized chart. The top pane shows stacked bars (ECT, Internal, ER shifts per month) while the bottom pane shows how many employees were active that month.

Monthly Overview — Stacked bars for shift types with active employee counts below
  • Top pane — Stacked bar segments colored by shift type. Hover a segment to see the exact count.
  • Bottom pane — Bar chart showing how many unique employees received assignments that month.
  • Legend — Right side shows shift type colors and the "Active Employees" metric.
  • What to look for — A healthy trend has consistent total shifts and a growing or stable employee base.

3. Shift Distribution

A nested donut chart showing the breakdown of shifts. The inner ring shows totals per shift type (ECT, Internal, ER). The outer ring drills into individual employees within each type.

Shift Distribution — Inner ring = shift types, outer ring = per-employee breakdown
  • Center — Shows total shift count across all types.
  • Inner ring — One segment per shift type, sized by count. Hover for percentage.
  • Outer ring — Click a type segment to drill down and see each employee's contribution.
  • What to look for — An even outer ring means shifts are spread fairly. One dominant segment means one employee is assigned disproportionately.

4. Shift Gap Analysis

A horizontal diverging bar chart centered on the team average. Bars extending right mean the employee has more shifts than average; bars extending left mean fewer.

Gap Analysis — Green = balanced, blue = over average, orange/red = under average or extreme
  • Center line (0) — The team average. Values like "+1.4" mean 1.4 shifts above average.
  • Color coding — Green (balanced, near zero), Blue (over average), Orange (under average), Red (extreme deviation).
  • What to look for — In a perfectly fair system, all bars would be near zero. Long red bars on either side need attention.

5. Fairness Over Time

A filled area chart tracking two metrics month by month: the overall Fairness Score (0–100%) and the Max-Min Gap (difference between the most and least assigned employees).

  • Fairness Score line — Higher is better. Green zone (80–100%), yellow (60–80%), red (<60%).
  • Max-Min Gap line — Lower is better. A rising gap means growing inequality.
  • What to look for — The fairness score should stay in the green zone. If it drops into yellow/red, the scheduling algorithm may need adjustment.

6. Employees Shifts Heatmap

A color-intensity grid with employees as rows and months as columns. Darker purple cells mean more shifts that month. Rows are sorted by total shift count (most to fewest).

Heatmap — Darker cells indicate more shifts. Dash (–) means zero shifts that month.
  • Color scale — Light (0–1 shifts) to dark purple (2+ shifts). A gradient legend appears at the top.
  • Total column — Rightmost column sums all months for each employee.
  • Total row — Bottom row sums all employees for each month.
  • What to look for — Uniformly medium-shaded cells across all rows = fair distribution. A row of dark cells = overworked employee. A row of dashes = underutilized.

Shift Exchange

Employees with linked accounts
Shift Exchange — Monthly calendar view with My Shifts, Incoming, Outgoing, and History tabs

How Shift Swaps Work

If you can't make a shift, you can request another employee to take it. The whole process happens in real-time with WebSocket notifications — no page refresh needed.

The Four Tabs

My Shifts

View your assigned shifts for the selected month. Click a shift to initiate a swap request.

Incoming

Swap requests from other employees asking you to take their shift. Accept or decline with one click. Badge shows count.

Outgoing

Requests you've sent to others. You can cancel pending requests here.

History

Completed and rejected exchange records for reference.

Step-by-Step Exchange Flow

Here's the complete flow for requesting and completing a shift swap:

  1. Step 1: Select your shift & browse candidates

    On the My Shifts tab, click the shift you want to swap. A Candidates panel slides open on the right showing every eligible colleague. Each candidate lists the dates they can offer in return — click any date pill (e.g. "15 Sun") to start a swap with that specific date.

    March 23 selected — 8 candidates available with their swappable dates

    The system automatically filters candidates using constraint rules: max 2 shifts per month, max 1 per week, no consecutive days, and form-declared availability. Employees tagged "New" are recent hires with limited scheduling windows.

  2. Step 2: Confirm the swap details

    After clicking a candidate's date, a Confirm Swap Request modal appears showing exactly which dates will be exchanged. You can optionally add a reason (e.g. "Family event") before sending.

    Confirm modal — Review the swap before sending

    Click "Send Request" to submit, or "Back" to choose a different candidate. The reason field is optional but helps the other employee understand why you're requesting the swap.

  3. Step 3: Track your request (Outgoing tab)

    After sending, switch to the Outgoing tab to see all your pending requests. Each card shows both sides of the swap — your shift and theirs — along with shift type badges (ECT, Internal, ER) and the reason you provided.

    Outgoing tab — Two pending requests with Cancel option

    You can cancel any pending request before the other person responds. The target employee receives a real-time notification via WebSocket.

  4. Step 4: Respond to requests (Incoming tab)

    When a colleague sends you a swap request, it appears in your Incoming tab with their reason. Each request shows the shift details for both sides with color-coded type badges, plus large Accept and Decline buttons.

    Incoming tab — Accept or Decline swap requests from colleagues

    The badge count on the tab (Incoming 2) shows how many unresolved requests are waiting. Accepting a swap immediately updates the schedule for both employees.

  5. Step 5: Review the audit trail (History tab)

    Every completed, declined, or cancelled request moves to the History tab with a color-coded status badge and timestamp. Declined requests also show the decline reason.

    History tab — Full audit trail of all exchange outcomes
    • Accepted — Swap completed, both schedules updated automatically
    • Declined — The other employee chose not to swap, with their stated reason
    • Cancelled — You withdrew the request before a response was given

Real-Time Notifications

The page uses a WebSocket connection (shown as "Live" or "Offline" in the header). When someone responds to your swap request, you'll see the update instantly without refreshing.

You must have your account linked to an employee record to use this page. If you see "no employee linked", ask an admin to link your account on the Employees page.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions and quick answers
Why can't I see certain pages in the sidebar?

Your admin controls which pages are visible to employees. If a page is missing, ask your admin to enable it in the Dashboard's "Page Access Control" section.

How is the Fairness Score calculated?

The fairness score measures how evenly shifts are distributed. It's based on the standard deviation of shift counts. A perfectly even distribution scores 100%.

What does "New Employee" mean for scheduling?

New employees (first month doing shifts) are restricted to only be assigned shifts in the last 2 weeks of the month. This allows them to settle in before taking shifts.

Can I undo a published assignment?

Publishing sends notifications but doesn't lock the data. An admin can re-run the assignment for the same month. However, employees who already added shifts to their calendar would need to update manually.

The chat says "rate limited" or "AI not available"

The AI service has daily usage limits. Wait a minute and try again, or try later in the day. If the issue persists, the admin may need to check the AI provider configuration.

How do I add my shifts to Google Calendar?

Go to your Profile page and click "Add to Calendar" next to each shift. This opens Google Calendar with the event pre-filled. You can also click calendar links from the notification bell.

Google Forms says "Not authenticated"

Sign out of the app and sign back in with Google. This refreshes the Google connection and re-grants the necessary permissions for Forms and Drive access.
